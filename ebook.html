Sex Work: The Perks & Drawbacks

Just to be clear: I am writing this book for people who are interested in learning more about the inner workings of sex work, whether out of curiosity or because they are considering giving it a try for themselves.

I will say that my own experience working as an escort for over ten years has been very positive and I have no regrets. I found the work very rewarding and exciting, for the most part.

But please remember to be prudent when reading this, as I am only one person who is also a sex worker. You can't assume that my experiences are the same as that of other sex workers and you shouldn't assume that yours would be the same either.

Please do not use this book as your sole research on the subject if you are seriously considering doing it yourself. It is always unwise to base any major decision on the advice of one person or by only looking at something from one angle.

That being said, let's take a look at the most attractive perks of sex work.

Amount of Freedom

This one is pretty predictable. It's the same reason why starting your own business or being a self-made entrepreneur is so alluring to most people – you get to work for yourself.

That means setting your own schedule, choosing your own hours, taking whatever time off that you want and can afford to, the ability to pick and choose who you work with, deciding what services you offer, and making whatever rates you set for your time.

Having this much control over the work you do makes escorting pretty intriguing to most people.

However, a lot of people fail to realize that having so much control demands certain personality traits and skills in order to succeed.

For example, I happen to be terrible when it comes to managing my time and I have little self-discipline when it comes to structuring my day or sticking to plans.

If I had been stronger in these areas, it would have helped me do even better as an escort.

Managing my spending and sticking to a budget are also skills I don't really have, and that hurt me as well.

If you are like me in these ways, you may still make a lot of money working as an escort, but you also will risk getting burnt out on it and it may end up feeling like you are maintaining rather than thriving.

On the other hand, if you kick ass when it comes to budgeting and have tons of self-discipline, then escorting can literally make things possible for you that never were before in your life.

You can pay your college tuition without getting swamped in student loan debts.

You can pay with cash to buy yourself a new car.

You can make a down payment on a house.

You can travel to anywhere in the world you want to.

You can provide for your children and family.

The list of possibilities is endless.

The Money

Yes, the money is a huge perk of working as an escort.

That's one client or two at most per day (if you get two half-hour bookings instead of one for an hour).

That would leave you with $1,400 per week and $5,600 per month.

Have I done that?

Yep.

The best part about this "1 hour a day" plan is that it's easy to do even if you currently have another day job.

Most people can spare an hour or two after work.

But imagine what an extra $67,200 per year would do to improve your financial situation.

The "Girlfriend Experience" Fantasy

One of the most often encountered acronyms in online escort communities and review boards is GFE, which stands for "girlfriend experience."

It's also the most often encountered fantasy, in my own experience.

Essentially, GFE translates to having sex the same way you would if you were dating someone and had an intimate relationship with them.

I've always found that more than a little strange, personally.

The opposite of GFE is PSE, which stands for "Porn Star Experience."

Contrary to what many would guess, this is by far the less common request in my experience.

They forget that you are there because they are paying you for your time.

Incall vs. Outcall Sessions

Another detail to work out if you plan on escorting is where you will meet with your clients.

In the escorting community, the term "incall" refers to clients coming to meet you at a location you provide for the session.

This can be your own residence, a hotel room or any other private place you can host.

Alternatively, "outcall" generally means you travel to the client and they host the session instead.

Be wary of meeting with new clients at hotels without a meet and greet or provider references, as most sting operations by law enforcement take place at hotels where escorts are lured and then arrested.

If you are going to offer incall, then you need a private space that is safe for hosting sessions. If you live alone and have sufficient privacy, using your home is certainly fine.

If you live in an apartment, it's riskier and should probably be avoided unless your particular setup has enough privacy to make it viable.

You don't want to attract any unwanted attention or have any nosy neighbors or property managers taking notice of all the traffic and different guys who are dropping in for short visits throughout the day.

This sends up red flags and creates suspicions that you need to do your best to avoid.

Of course, the biggest benefit to offering incall is that you save a lot of time you would otherwise spend travelling to your clients.

You save a lot of time, actually.

After a session is over, you can tell your next client to head over and then jump in the shower and be ready by the time they arrive instead of having to drive to their house after you get out.

It might not seem significant, but it eats up more time than you might think it would.
